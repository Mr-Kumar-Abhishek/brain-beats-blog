### The 440 Hz Standard: A Modern Tuning with Purpose

Since the mid‑20th century, **440 Hz** has been internationally adopted as the standard pitch for the note A above middle C. This tuning is used across most Western music, from orchestras and pop songs to film scores and digital instruments.

*   **Characteristics:** 440 Hz is often described as bright, precise, and intellectually stimulating. It carries a “modern” energy that reflects the structured, technological world we live in.
*   **Comparison to 432 Hz:** While 432 Hz is celebrated for its natural, heart‑centered warmth, 440 Hz offers a different quality—clarity, alertness, and a sense of order. For tasks that require sharp mental focus and logical thinking, 440 Hz can be particularly effective.

Rather than viewing one tuning as “better” than the other, it’s more useful to see them as tools for different purposes. 440 Hz is the tuning of achievement, precision, and the modern pace—qualities that resonate deeply with Capricorn’s driven personality.
