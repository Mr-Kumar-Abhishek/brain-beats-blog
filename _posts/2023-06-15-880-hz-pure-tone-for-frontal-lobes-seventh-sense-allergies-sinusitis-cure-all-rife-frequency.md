## What Are Rife Frequencies?

Rife frequency therapy originates from the work of **Dr. Royal Raymond Rife** in the early 20th century. Rife proposed that every microorganism—bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites—vibrates at a specific natural frequency, much like a musical instrument has a specific pitch. By exposing a pathogen to its exact resonant frequency, he believed it was possible to cause the microorganism to vibrate intensely enough to rupture its structure, thereby neutralizing it without damaging surrounding healthy tissue.

Although Rife's methods were controversial and never adopted by mainstream medicine, the concept of using targeted frequencies for wellness has persisted in alternative and complementary health circles. Modern Rife practice often involves listening to pure tones or complex waveforms, with the idea that these sound vibrations can interact with the body's cells and energy fields to promote balance, support immune function, and address various health concerns.

## 880 Hz as the Higher Octave of 13.8 Hz: The Cognitive Connection

One of the foundational aspects of 880 Hz is its mathematical link to **13.8 Hz**. In music and physics, an **octave** represents a doubling of frequency. Starting from 13.8 Hz:

*   13.8 Hz × 2 = 27.6 Hz (1st octave)
*   27.6 Hz × 2 = 55.2 Hz (2nd octave)
*   55.2 Hz × 2 = 110.4 Hz (3rd octave)
*   110.4 Hz × 2 = 220.8 Hz (4th octave)
*   220.8 Hz × 2 = 441.6 Hz (5th octave)
*   441.6 Hz × 2 = 883.2 Hz ≈ **880 Hz** (6th octave)

Thus, 880 Hz is approximately **six octaves higher** than 13.8 Hz. This relationship is significant because **13.8 Hz sits at the border between the alpha (8‑13 Hz) and low‑beta (14‑30 Hz) brainwave ranges**. Alpha waves are associated with relaxed alertness and meditation, while low‑beta waves are linked to focused attention, problem‑solving, and conscious thought.
